Requirement Model

The exact, deterministic scoring that turns an item's roll into a level and stat demand. Every number below is a slider in the addon's settings.

Power Score

Item power (defaults)
base = { White:0, Magic:2, Rare:6, Legendary:20, Relic:42 } power = base + 1 per affix + 7 extra per Greater affix + 12 extra per Artifact affix + (Corrupted ? 4 : 0) + (Cleansed ? 6 : 0) + (FactionRelic ? 6 : 0) # jewelry: Sanctified +6, Desecrated -6 level = ceil( max(power, highest socketed rune floor) · 0.6 ) # 0.6 = the global scale slider - one knob to soften or harshen everything

White and plain items score 0 and demand nothing. Magic items ask only a token level; the curve steepens hard through Legendary and Relic, and Greater / Artifact ranks are what really price an item up.

Stat demands

Higher-power items also demand attributes. The primary stat is keyed to the item's role; a secondary stat kicks in once power reaches 25. Both pass through the same global 0.6 scale.

Stat requirements (defaults)
primary = ceil( (5 + ceil(power · 0.65)) · 0.6 ) secondary = ceil( (5 + ceil(power · 0.35)) · 0.6 ) # only when power ≥ 25 mythic wis = ceil( (5 + ceil(power · 0.41)) · 0.6 ) # Mythics only
Item typePrimary → Secondary
Ranged weaponDexterity → Strength
Melee weaponStrength → Dexterity
ApparelVitality → Strength
JewelryWisdom → Charisma

Special cases

  • Uniques use a flat base of 50, Mythics 66 (modifiers still stack), and Mythics add the tertiary Wisdom demand - "Mythic Attunement," because a god-forged artifact needs a mind strong enough to hold it.
  • Runes: a rune's level floor is ceil(ladderIndex × 1.3), folded into the item's level as a floor before the global scale - Diablo 2 rules, never additive. Gems use ceil(index × 2.5) but are hard-capped at level 20, so they stay the everyman's socketable. Facet jewels ask a flat 25.
  • Jewelry: worn pieces use tier base plus rung bonuses (Lesser through Divine), Sanctified +6, Desecrated -6; charms are level-only and gate at attunement rather than equip.
Worked example. A corrupted Legendary greatsword with 5 affixes, two of them Greater, scores 20 + 5 + 2×7 + 4 = 43. Its demands: Level ceil(43×0.6) = 26, Strength ceil((5+28)×0.6) = 20, and - power ≥ 25 - Dexterity ceil((5+16)×0.6) = 13. A genuine endgame blade priced like one.

Enforcement

A colonist who doesn't meet an item's demands simply cannot equip or wear it - the attempt is blocked with the reason spelled out. The gate sits on the equip path itself, so it holds everywhere:

  • Vanilla equip and wear orders, outfit policies, and AI pickup.
  • True RPG Inventory - drag-to-slot and float-menu Wear / Equip are gated at the source.
  • Simple Sidearms - taking a sidearm into a weapon slot is checked the same way.
Nothing is stored. The score is recomputed on demand, so re-rolling, corrupting, or cleansing an item instantly updates its requirement. Calculations are fail-open: a bug can never break equipping.
