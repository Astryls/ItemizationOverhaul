The Cleansing

The endgame ritual: reforge a corrupted item into a relic bonded to a colonist, carrying one of twelve double-edged blessings.

The ritual

Only a corrupted item can be Cleansed. The act consumes ultra-rare Holy Water, bonds the item to a chosen colonist, and reforges it into a relic of your own ideology. In exchange for shedding the corruption it gains one of twelve blessings - each a powerful boon with a real cost.

Bonding. A cleansed item becomes a persona-style bonded relic - the cleansing is the only bonding path in the suite, since vanilla persona / bladelink weapons spawn inert. The item shows "Relic of [your ideology]" everywhere, with an animated gold pulse outline.

The twelve blessings

Every blessing is a numeric wearer effect - a strong upside paired with a matching downside. The blessing is rolled when the item is cleansed.

Spectral - the special case

Spectral does more than a stat swap: it sets the item Ethereal (+50% base stats) and overcharges every affix by 50% with no cap - integer affixes can exceed their normal hard ceilings. The price is a permanent -50% to the item's hit points, offset by the item becoming self-repairing.

Worked example. You corrupt a Relic axe (gaining a unique), then cleanse it. It rolls Bloodforged: +60% melee damage, but now weighs twice as much - so the colonist who bonds it should be a strong melee bruiser, not a runner. The axe is now a relic of your ideology, appears on your faith's page, and gave your colony a small mood lift for the act.

Mood. Giving up a relic to cleanse it reads as "We gave up a sacred relic…" - the ritual is a deliberate, weighty choice, not a free upgrade.
