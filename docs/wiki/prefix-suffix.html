Prefix & Suffix Table

The full curated affix vocabulary — every authored word, the stat it grants, and the tier it starts at.

A magic-or-better item names itself from its affixes in the Diablo tongue: a prefix is the leading adjective, a suffix is the trailing "of…" clause. A Magic item reads [Prefix] [Base] of [Suffix] — e.g. Cruel Longsword of the Muffalo. Rare and Legendary items instead earn a generated two-word title, but they still roll from the same word pool below.

Tier = earliest appearance. The tier column is the lowest rarity a word can roll at; it remains available at every higher tier unless it is a Rare-only word. Marquee combat/defence stats use escalating word ladders so the name signals the power.

Beyond the catalogue

The words above are the curated vocabulary. Any eligible pawn stat the catalogue doesn't cover — including stats added by other mods — is auto-synthesized into the pool with a heuristic name, so modded content still rolls affixes. Stats where lower is better (aiming time, cooldowns, incoming damage…) automatically roll as a reduction. See Affixes & Ranks for how values, Greater and Artifact upgrades scale.
