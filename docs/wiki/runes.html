Sockets in one minute

Every weapon and apparel that the Core rolls can roll sockets (40% chance on non-set gear, weighted by slot). A socket holds one rune or one facet jewel, drawn from a shared budget. Sockets roll once at item creation and are locked forever - what an item rolled is what it keeps. Plain (un-enchanted) items roll zero sockets, honouring the Core drop rate.

Runes have no tier of their own

A rune's effect depends entirely on where you socket it. Brim in a weapon ignites enemies; in body armor it is +12% heat armor. The 30 runes climb a rarity ladder (Normal → Rare → Legendary → Relic) that only describes how rare they are to find, never their power.

Getting runes

  • Combat drops - 4% chance (tunable) per hostile kill; odds fall 20% per ladder step, so a top rune is roughly 1 in 660.
  • Carving - the ten lowest runes can be carved at a smithy from steel + components.
  • Ascension - any three identical runes fuse into one of the next ladder step, all the way to the top.

Suite integration

  • Sets - set items get a standardized socket count per set and slot (no RNG).
  • Isekai Leveling - shares one socket budget with the Runic Station; refinement never adds slots.
  • Jewelry - rings, amulets and charms never take sockets (D2-faithful).
Permanent choices. Socketing cannot be undone - choose like it is Diablo 2 and you just found your first high rune.
