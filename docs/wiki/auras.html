Auras

A rare affix that radiates a constant effect and a halo of floating motes. The showpiece of the affix system - at most one per item.

The ten base auras

An aura affix grants a passive field around the wearer. Defensive auras buff the bearer; the Holy auras pulse damage at nearby enemies. Each renders its own coloured mote field.

AuraEffect

Meditation+Meditation focus gain

The Holy (damage) auras are the rarest of all auras in the roll.

How auras apply

Aura stat bonuses are injected at the central stat finalize step, so they show up everywhere - the bio stats page, the gear tab, and in live combat alike. A Defiance helmet's armor bonus appears on the pawn's armor stat; a Vigor item's speed appears on Move Speed.

Aura potency scaling
magnitude = roll[0.10 .. 0.25] · potencyMultiplier Greater ★ -> ×1.2 Artifact ✦ -> ×1.5

Damage auras pulse on a timer against enemies inside the aura radius; the wiki's uber auras hit harder and add a deeper crowd-control field.

The three corruption-only elemental auras

Three overwhelming elemental auras exist only through corruption - they can never roll naturally:

AuraElementWhat it does
RadianceFireA blazing damage field, far stronger than Holy Fire.
EntropyColdDeep chill: a two-stage Chilled hediff that can crater enemy move speed.
SuperconductorLightningA storm field that arcs through nearby foes.

These, along with the seven named single-aura uniques (Swiftness, Bulwark, Crusader's Might, and the rest), render a distinct stylized emblem instead of the base mote.

Worked example

Entropy on a corrupted relic. A relic-tier breastplate is corrupted and rolls the Entropy unique. Now every hostile that steps inside its field gains the Chilled hediff - at high intensity it deepens to "entropic," dropping their move speed by up to 2.4. Pair it with a cold facet jewel in the Runes addon and the field's damage is amplified on top.

Tuning brightness

Auras render in two independently-scalable layers, both controlled from Mod settings → Visuals → Auras and both ranging 0–200% (default 100%):

Aura glow brightness
The ground glow disc and the spinning rune beneath the pawn.
Aura particle brightness
The floating orbs and elemental particles (embers, snow, sparks).

Set either to 0% to hide that layer completely — it isn't drawn at all, so it costs nothing. Turn the particles down if the orbiting motes are distracting but you still want the ground rune to mark your aura-bearers.

Aura art is tinted in-game. The mote emblems ship as white / greyscale textures and are coloured at runtime by the glow material, so each aura reads at a glance by its hue.
