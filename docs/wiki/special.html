Ethereal & Indestructible

Two special modifiers that roll independently of the affix budget. An item can have at most one.

Ethereal

An Ethereal item is translucent and supercharged. It grants +50% to its real base stats - armor, insulation, and ranged damage multiplier - on top of whatever affixes it rolled. The trade-off in classic ARPG fashion: it cannot be repaired.

  • The +50% applies through the same stat pipeline as base-stat variance, so it stacks multiplicatively with the item's own ±15% roll.
  • Ethereal also boosts every affix value by 50% when the item is rolled - including Greater affixes (Artifact values are left at their fixed over-cap numbers).
  • The item renders at reduced opacity (tunable in settings, 50 - 90%).
Worked example. An Ethereal Legendary cataphract helmet with a base 1.08 sharp armor and a +9% variance roll shows roughly 1.08 × 1.09 × 1.5 ≈ 1.77 sharp armor before affixes - and its +Sharp Armor affix is itself 50% larger. A monster of a helmet that you must protect, because it can never be mended.

Indestructible

An Indestructible item never loses hit points and never deteriorates - it will outlast your colony. White items never roll it; the chance rises with tier.

Roll odds

Both modifiers roll on an independent chance, scaled by tier, separate from the affix count. They are mutually exclusive - a combined "ethereal & indestructible" item is reserved for later systems.

TierSpecial chance (each)
White2%
Rare6%
Legendary15%
Indestructiblenever on White; rolls on the tier chance above
Etherealrolls on the tier chance; if both land, one is dropped at random

At the Luminary Cube

You can make any item Indestructible at the Luminary Cube. Ethereal arrives naturally on rolls and through one of the twelve cleansing blessings (Spectral, which also overcharges affixes uncapped).
