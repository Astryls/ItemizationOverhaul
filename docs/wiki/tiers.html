Loot Tiers

A Diablo-style rarity ladder - White, Magic, Rare, Legendary - plus the apex Relic flag. The tier sets the affix budget, the naming style, and what every other system is allowed to roll.

The rarity ladder

Affixes are split into prefix and suffix slots (D2-style). Each tier rolls a number of each; the table shows prefix + suffix budgets.

White carries no affixes at all - only natural ±15% base-stat variance (see Base-stat variance). A truly plain (un-enchanted) drop keeps the bare vanilla item; an enchanted White still shows its base-stat range on the card. Higher tiers bias their stat affixes toward a curated "premium" pool of impactful stats.

Tier naming

Each tier above White generates a Diablo-flavoured name, and the name pool is type-aware - weapon-flavoured words (Ripper, Cleaver, Edge) never land on armor, and armor words (Aegis, Bulwark, Crest) never land on weapons. Shields are apparel, so they keep armor words like "Aegis".

  • Magic - "[Prefix] [Base] of [Suffix]", built from its two affixes (e.g. Cruel Plate of the Bear).
  • Rare - a D2-style two-word name (e.g. Gore Ripper, Storm Crest).
  • Legendary - a D4-style grand title (e.g. The Ashen Aegis, Fang of the Tempest, Sunderer's Requiem).

Spawn odds

Each item that rolls enchanted (see Affix Drop Rate) draws a tier. The chances are cumulative; whatever is left over after Legendary, Rare and Magic falls through to White.

Tier roll (per enchanted item)
r = random[0,1) if r < chanceLegendary -> Legendary elif r < chanceLegendary + chanceRare -> Rare elif r < chanceLegendary + chanceRare + chanceMagic -> Magic else -> White relic? = (tier == Legendary) and random < relicChance
chanceLegendarydefault 0.05 - top rolled tier; gates Relic and the Unique overlay
chanceRaredefault 0.15
chanceMagicdefault 0.35
relicChancedefault 0.03 - chance a Legendary drop is promoted to Relic

All four are sliders under the Drop rates & tiers tab of Options → Mod settings → Itemization Overhaul - Core (the panel shows the leftover White % live). Raise them for a magic-item-rich game; lower them for a lean, vanilla-leaning feel.

The Relic tier

Relic is not a fifth rung in the roll - it is a flag set on top of a Legendary base. A Relic item always carries 7 affixes, can roll Artifact ✦ potency, wears a distinctive rose frame with an animated sparkle, and gains a generated artwork. Relics are also the only items that can become faction relics or carry corruption-only unique powers at full strength.

Worked example. An enchanted drop rolls r = 0.03, below chanceLegendary (0.05), so it is Legendary: 2-3 prefixes and 2-3 suffixes, each with a chance to turn Greater, under a grand title. A further relicChance roll succeeds, promoting it to Relic - now it locks to 7 affixes, several may turn Artifact, it grows an artwork, and it could be a faction's coveted relic.

How tiers show up

  • Item card - a coloured tier banner (grey / blue / yellow / orange), or "Relic" for relic-tier.
  • On the ground - the tier word replaces the vanilla quality overlay; nothing shows for plain items.
  • Glow & stars - higher tiers get a subtle coloured glow and, with Greater affixes, gold ★ stars on the label.
Tier gates the whole suite. Item sets only roll on Legendary or Relic; the gold Unique overlay requires Legendary; level requirements scale from the tier. Lower the tier odds and the entire suite naturally produces fewer special items.
