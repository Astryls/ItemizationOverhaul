Slots & Charms

How the neck and ring slots fit alongside armor, and how the inventory attunement budget works.

Worn jewelry

SlotRules
AmuletOne neck-layer slot, invisible when worn - never fights with clothing or armor.
RingsTwo slots, one per hand. Equipping a third is refused at equip time.

Worn jewelry inherits the full Core machinery - tier, corruption, cleansing, faction relics, and Stats & Levels requirements - while carrying only the exclusive jewelry affix pool.

Charms - power from the pack

Charms are carved talismans that work from a pawn's inventory - no slot needed. But attention is finite: each pawn has an attunement budget (default 6 points).

CharmAttunement cost
Small charm1 point
Large charm2 points
Grand charm3 points

The strongest charms are attuned first until the budget runs out; charms beyond the budget are carried but inert. Charms roll the same Core tiers but carry only the jewelry affix pool, and they remain enchanted regardless of the drop rate - they are dedicated rare drops.

Single-carry legends

The two mythic charms - The Archonexus Shard and Torch of the Burning Throne - are single-carry: a pawn may bear only one of each identity. A second copy is refused on the ground and in the float menu, the inspect line reads "Only one may be borne," and the attunement system never attunes a duplicate. Both carry bespoke art set at spawn.

Worked example. A pawn carries a Grand charm (3 pts) and a Large charm (2 pts) - 5 of 6 points used. A second Grand charm (3 pts) would overflow the budget, so it stays inert in the pack until you drop something. Swap the Large for a Small (1 pt) and now both Grands could attune. Budget management is the charm game.
