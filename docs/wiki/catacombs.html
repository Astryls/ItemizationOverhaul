Catacombs

An endlessly-descending Diablo dungeon built on RimWorld's own layout engine - unique procedural crypts per floor, deadlier and richer the deeper you delve.

Itemization Overhaul - Catacombs

The pitch

Research Catacomb Delving and build a catacombs entrance - a reinforced stone stairwell that bores into the deep rock. Send a party down and they descend through procedurally generated crypts, each floor harder than the last, the loot richer the deeper you go.

How a delve works

  1. Build the catacombs entrance and send a party of colonists down.
  2. Each floor is a unique generated crypt. Find the descending stairs to delve deeper, or the ascending stairs to climb back.
  3. Guardians grow stronger every floor; the spoils grow richer to match. See Delving & Floors.
  4. Crack open sealed sarcophagi for grave-goods - richer the deeper you are.
  5. Open a town portal to snap home; a matching portal appears beside your entrance so you can return to that exact depth later.

Built on vanilla tech. Floors use RimWorld's structure-layout and map-gen systems rather than a bespoke generator, so they feel native and stay compatible.

Suite synergy

The catacombs are the suite's content faucet: a renewable, scalable source of unidentified loot, runes, set pieces, and relics. Pair them with Scrolls & Potions - Town Portal scrolls for emergency extraction, Identify scrolls for the unidentified haul, and Health potions for the fights.

Worked example. A three-colonist party delves to floor 6. The crypt stone has darkened with depth; a spelopede hive guards the descending stairs. They clear it, crack two sarcophagi for an unidentified Legendary and a rune, then open a town portal home to identify the haul - returning later via the paired surface portal to push for floor 7.
