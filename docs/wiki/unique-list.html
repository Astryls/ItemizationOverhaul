Unique Compendium

Every one of the 34 named Uniques, with its fixed affixes and lore.

All 34 named Uniques, grouped by slot family. Each manifests on any Relic-tier item of its slot (vanilla or modded) and always rolls these exact affixes. ★ = Greater, ✦ = Artifact.

Head

Crest of the Laughing CourtHead
  • +2 to all skills ★
  • +35% Learning ★
  • +30% Psychic Sensitivity
  • −6% Break Threshold

On a glitterworld whose name is now a legal liability, the Laughing Court advised seven planetary governors through jokes alone. Their crest was stitched for the one fool who outlived all seven - by always knowing which joke to leave untold. Wearers report that every problem looks slightly funnier, and therefore slightly smaller.

Crown of the DeathlessHead
  • +20% Pain Threshold ★
  • +12% Sharp Armor
  • +25% Immunity Gain
  • Aura - Prayer (strength 0.35)

Urbworld embalmers crowned their dead kings with circlets like this one, wired to keep the brainstem warm against the day medicine caught up with ambition. This is the only one recovered with no skull inside. Whoever wore it first never took it off; whoever wears it next will understand why.

Gaze of the SanguophageHead
  • +18% Blunt Armor ★
  • +15% Pain Threshold
  • +40% Rest Rate
  • +15% Melee Damage

A sanguophage elder wore this through four centuries of polite society, and the lenses learned what it watched the world with. They were ground from something that was not glass and did not want to be a lens. In the dark, the wearer's sleep grows strangely efficient - as if rest were being deposited by an outside party.

Mane of the Wild SpeakerHead
  • +20% Tame Chance ★
  • +20% Train Chance
  • +25% Psychic Sensitivity
  • +0.25 Move Speed

The wild speakers of the northern tribes braid their headdress from the mane of every animal that chose to stay. This one carries four hundred years of choosing. Thrumbos lower their horns when the wearer approaches - not in challenge, witnesses are careful to note, but in greeting.

Visage of the Venom QueenHead
  • +50% Toxic Resistance ★
  • +30% Immunity Gain ★
  • +5 Melee Hit Chance
  • +10% Work Speed

A death-cult on a toxic world crowned their queen with a mask grown from cultured blackvenom chitin. She outlived four generations of her own poisoners, smiling through every meal. The mask remembers what it filtered, and shares the trick.

The Valkyrie's PinionHead
  • +4 Melee Hit Chance ★
  • +0.40 Move Speed
  • +3 Shooting Accuracy

Imperial death-singers wear a single wing into the drop pod: the promise that someone chooses who falls and who is carried home. This pinion belonged to a cataphract who made that choice eleven hundred times and was never once seen to miss. The wing motif is not decorative. It is a kept promise.

Outer armor

ShaftbreakerOuter armor
  • +30% Blunt Armor ✦
  • +15% Pain Threshold
  • +25 Carrying Capacity

Deep-core mining colonies tell of the collapse at Shaft Nineteen: three kilometers of rock coming down, and one figure walking out of the dust carrying two of the diggers who didn't. The armor kept the name the miners gave it. Assayers note the plating has been compressed to a density the original manufacturer never specified.

The Burrowlord's ShellOuter armor
  • +20% Sharp Armor ★
  • −25°C min comfort temp ★
  • +15°C max comfort temp
  • +20% Immunity Gain

Grown, not forged - cut whole from the carapace of an insectoid matriarch the deep-drillers called the Burrowlord, who kept her hive at a temperature that froze the sap in men's boots. The chitin still flexes when the temperature drops, as if remembering colder tunnels and the things that throve in them.

Plate of the Sunken LeviathanOuter armor
  • +28% Sharp Armor ✦
  • +15% Blunt Armor
  • +20 Carrying Capacity
  • −0.25 Move Speed

An ocean world's salvage guilds spent a generation cutting plate from the hull of something vast that had settled into the trench - ship or creature, the guild records refuse to commit. The armor is heavy as a sea and twice as patient. Wearers describe the weight as "company."

The Widow's WailOuter armor
  • +20% Work Speed ★
  • +20% Learning
  • +6 Melee Dodge
  • Aura - Meditation (strength 0.40)

A widowed artisan on a forgotten rimworld wove this over the eleven years after the raid that took her family, and she put the years in with the thread. It does not make the wearer fearless; it makes the wearer finish. The lining hums very softly when the wearer is close to giving up, and then they don't.

The Guardian's PromiseOuter armor
  • +22% Sharp Armor ★
  • +22% Heat Armor ★
  • +18% Pain Threshold
  • Aura - Defiance (strength 0.35)

A bodyguard on a glitterworld swore to bring her principal home through the riot, the fire, and the orbital strike - in that order, in one night. The armor kept the oath after she stopped being able to. It has been keeping it for somebody ever since.

Clothing

Skin of the VipermageClothing
  • +40% Psychic Sensitivity ★
  • +30% Entropy Recovery
  • +20% Heat Armor
  • +25% Meditation Focus

The vipermages of the eastern rim bonded serpent-leather to their own nervous systems to feel psychic weather coming, the way old sailors feel storms in a knee. This is a shed skin - theirs or the serpent's, scholars argue. The scales shift color with the wearer's pulse. Psycasters pay fortunes for it; the wise ones pay quietly.

The Twitching WeaveClothing
  • +10 Melee Dodge ★
  • +12% Work Speed
  • −10% Aiming Delay

Its threads were pulled taut on a loom run by a malfunctioning persona core that believed it was being hunted. The paranoia got into the cloth. The garment is always slightly ahead of you - sleeves arriving where your arms are about to be, hems leaning out of the way of blades you haven't seen yet.

Corvid's CryClothing
  • +4 Shooting Accuracy ★
  • +0.30 Move Speed
  • +30% Hunting Stealth

Tribal hunters of the northern taiga sew a ruff of corvid feathers for whichever of them the ravens choose to follow - the birds know who leaves the most behind. The garment is feather-dark and silent. Game animals look straight through the wearer; so, occasionally, do friends.

Wisdom of the HegemonClothing
  • +35% Research Speed ★
  • +30% Learning ★
  • +20% Entropy Recovery
  • −5% Break Threshold

The Hegemon of a five-system reach ruled for ninety years and was wrong, by the archive's count, twice. His robe was woven with a thread-thin lattice of archotech filament that did nothing at all, the weavers insisted, except listen. Scholars who wear it report that answers arrive slightly before the questions finish forming.

Legwear

The Sandstrider's TreksLegwear
  • +0.55 Move Speed ✦
  • +15% Immunity Gain
  • +15 Carrying Capacity

The sandstriders run mail across the extreme deserts - water, antibiotics, last letters. The greatest of them crossed the Anvil in nine days during the year of the red dust, and these are what she crossed it in. Witnesses at the far side swear the footprints in the sand were walking the other way.

Megaspider SilkweaveLegwear
  • +40% Hunting Stealth ★
  • +0.35 Move Speed ★
  • +25% Rest Rate

Harvesting silk from a living megaspider is done exactly once per harvester, one way or the other. This bolt was woven by hands that never once trembled, and the steadiness stayed in the weave. It makes no sound at all when the wearer moves. None.

The Gorerider's GreavesLegwear
  • +5 Melee Hit Chance ★
  • +12% Melee Damage
  • +0.25 Move Speed

Cavalry gear from the war over the thrumbo calving grounds - a war nobody won and nobody who was there discusses. The riders who wore these charged through what the chroniclers politely call "the red field." The stains predate every documented owner, and no solvent on the rim has an opinion about them.

The TidewalkersLegwear
  • +0.45 Move Speed ★
  • +30% Rest Rate
  • +20°C max comfort temp
  • +10% Immunity Gain

Ocean-world couriers run the tide flats between island colonies, racing the water out and back. The greatest of them was never once caught by the returning sea, and her crew swore the foam slowed down to let her pass. The salt never dries out of the weave, and neither does the hurry.

Utility

Razorspine GirdleUtility
  • +5 Shooting Accuracy ★
  • −15% Aiming Delay ★
  • +0.20 Move Speed

A belt of overlapping blades taken from the spine of a mechanoid scyther that an outlander duelist defeated, disassembled, and wore as a warning. Each blade is balanced for throwing and none has ever been thrown. It keeps the hands steady instead - the belt remembers precision the way scar tissue remembers the knife.

The Webspinner's MeshUtility
  • +30% Meditation Focus ★
  • +35% Psychic Sensitivity
  • +15% Work Speed
  • +25% Entropy Recovery

Spun by an archotech-touched recluse who lived forty years in a cave at the anima grove's edge, weaving and listening. The mesh tightens when the wearer concentrates, and the world obligingly recedes. Scholars who strap it on stop noticing meals, sunsets, decades.

The Trophy StringUtility
  • +25% Pain Threshold ★
  • +10% Sharp Armor
  • +10% Blunt Armor
  • −10% Social Impact

A pirate warlord's tally, worn at the hip: one ear per raid survived, knotted in gut-cord. It is exactly what it looks like. The string teaches the wearer what pain is worth and what it isn't, which is a genuine education. Polite company disagrees, from a distance.

GlitterwrapUtility
  • +15% Trade Prices ★
  • +25% Negotiation
  • +20% Social Impact
  • +10 Carrying Capacity

Glitterworld finance wears soft clothing with hard intentions, and this sash closed mergers that redrew orbital charts. The gold thread is real; the cut is calculated to the millimeter of envy. Merchants find themselves agreeing with the wearer before the haggling starts, and remember it afterward as their own idea.

The Stormgod's VigorUtility
  • +20% Pain Threshold ★
  • +0.30 Move Speed
  • +15% Heat Armor
  • Aura - Vigor (strength 0.40) ★

The storm-priests buried their highest with a belt of charged capacitor cells, so the dead would have lightning to spend in the next life. Grave-robbers learned, expensively, that the belt prefers the living. It hums against the wearer's spine like weather deciding where to happen.

Melee weapon

The ForefatherMelee weapon
  • +30% Melee Damage ✦
  • +8 Melee Hit Chance ★
  • +1 to all skills
  • +10% Pain Threshold

Passed down a line of warriors so long the family name wore away before the edge did. Each bearer added one notch to the tang and one rule to the teaching; there are one hundred and six notches and nobody alive knows all the rules. The blade fights like it has someone to disappoint.

The DoomsayerMelee weapon
  • +25% Melee Damage ★
  • +8 Melee Dodge
  • +0.20 Move Speed
  • Aura - Might (strength 0.45) ★

Forged in a fallen colony's last hour by a smith who knew exactly how the hour would end, and quenched in reactor coolant as the sirens wound down. The colony's name is stamped on the ricasso, struck through. It swings heavier at the end of things, and it always knows when the end of things is.

The WeatherbreakerMelee weapon
  • +10 Melee Hit Chance ✦
  • +15% Melee Damage
  • +20 Carrying Capacity
  • Aura - Fanaticism (strength 0.35)

The storm-priests of the eastern rim taught that every blow has a rhythm and the rhythm is called the weather. This weapon arrives a half-beat before its own swing - thunder first, lightning after, which is wrong, which is the point. Standing near a practice session reportedly affects barometers.

The Crimson ScalpelMelee weapon
  • +6 Melee Hit Chance ★
  • +0.35 Move Speed
  • +10% Work Speed

An urbworld surgeon of terrible genius scaled her favorite instrument up past all decency, on the reasoning that some patients arrive armored and uncooperative. It knows where everything important is. In her clinic's ledger, every entry under this tool reads the same: "procedure successful, patient discharged."

The Starfall HammerMelee weapon
  • +20% Melee Damage ★
  • +6 Melee Hit Chance
  • +15% Pain Threshold
  • Aura - HolyShock (strength 0.40) ★

Forged from a meteoric core that struck a smithy and, by every account, apologized. The metal still carries orbital charge; each impact discharges a little of the sky it fell out of. The smith who worked it kept her hair tied back and her funeral pre-paid.

Ranged weapon

The WindreaperRanged weapon
  • +8 Shooting Accuracy ✦
  • −25% Aiming Delay ★
  • +0.20 Move Speed

The shot leaves before the trigger finishes its travel - armorers have stripped it to the last pin and found nothing that explains the timing. Veteran snipers of three planetary militias pass down the same observation: when this weapon fires, the wind holds its breath to watch.

The Blizzard CannonRanged weapon
  • +5 Shooting Accuracy ★
  • −10% Aiming Delay
  • +10% Sharp Armor
  • +15 Carrying Capacity

Off-world catalogs listed it as a cold-climate engineering tool, which was technically true and morally negotiable. The ice-world mercenaries who carried it called it the Driller, for what it did to barricades, bulkheads, and arguments. The manufacturer's plate has been filed off - which is itself a kind of signature.

The HellburnerRanged weapon
  • +25% Heat Armor ★
  • +4 Shooting Accuracy
  • Aura - HolyFire (strength 0.40) ★

Recovered from a weapons bunker that had been on fire, by every forensic measure, for sixty years. It runs hot. It has always run hot. The grips are scorched in the shape of older hands, and new owners find their own grip drifting to match.

The CliffbreakerRanged weapon
  • +6 Shooting Accuracy ★
  • +35% Hunting Stealth
  • +0.25 Move Speed

Named for the shot that ended the siege of Redstone Mesa from the next biome over - through a sandstorm, witnesses add, though witnesses add a lot of things. Militia range tables refuse to account for it, and the rangemaster who tried was reassigned for his own peace of mind.

The Hexwoven StringRanged weapon
  • +5 Shooting Accuracy ★
  • −12% Aiming Delay
  • +30% Hunting Stealth
  • +10% Immunity Gain

A hedge-witch on a forgotten rimworld wove her own hair into a bowstring as payment for a debt the chronicle declines to specify. Arrows loosed from it arrive with opinions. Sickness avoids the bearer's camp; so, increasingly, does bad luck, which has somewhere better to be.
