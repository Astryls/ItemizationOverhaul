Crafting & Config

How a master smith forges better loot, and how to switch any individual affix on or off.

Skill-scaled crafting

When a colonist crafts a weapon or apparel, its tier is rolled from the crafter's relevant skill instead of the world drop table. A higher skill shifts the odds toward Rare and Legendary, and a true master has a rare shot at a Relic.

Crafted tier odds (by skill)
relicChance = skill ≥ 18 ? min(0.06, (skill-17) · 0.0025) : 0 legendary = clamp( (skill-10)/14 · 0.55 + min(0.3,(skill-20)·0.015) ) rare = min( (skill-4)/12 · 0.7 , 1 - legendary ) # remainder rolls White
  • Crafted gear is always enchanted - the drop-rate knob only thins world drops, never your own work.
  • Endless-growth friendly: skill levels above 20 (from leveling mods) keep nudging the odds upward, and the Relic chance climbs toward its 6% cap.
Worked example. A Crafting-20 smith forges a longsword: roughly a coin-flip for Legendary, a solid chance of Rare, and about a 0.75% shot at a Relic. Push that smith to skill 25 with an endless-growth mod and Legendary becomes the norm, with the Relic chance creeping toward 2%.

The per-affix configuration window

Open Options → Mod settings → Itemization Overhaul - Core and click "Configure individual affixes…" to open a searchable window where every affix can be toggled on or off. Disabled affixes never roll, on any item, anywhere.

The window groups affixes into collapsible categories, supports search and per-row descriptions, and has All / None / Reset controls. Addons register their own entries through the same API, so the one window governs the whole suite.

Use it to curate. Hate a particular aura, or want a pure-melee game with no ranged affixes? Switch them off here and they vanish from every future roll without touching anything else.
