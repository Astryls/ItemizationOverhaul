Create Items via XML

Add your own Uniques, Mythics, Sets, Runes, Gems, and Runewords with nothing but a Defs folder - no C# required. This is the same format the suite's own content uses.

The one-minute setup

Make a tiny mod folder with an About/About.xml and a Defs/ folder, and load it after the addon whose content you are extending:

About/About.xml (the relevant part)
<loadAfter>
  <li>astryl.ItemizationOverhaulCore</li>
  <li>astryl.ItemizationOverhaulUniques</li>  <!-- or Runes / Sets, whichever you extend -->
</loadAfter>

Drop any of the defs below into a file under Defs/ (the file name doesn't matter). The addon scans its DefDatabase at startup, so your content joins the same pools as the built-in legends - drop tables, expedition rewards, cube dev tools, everything.

Players count as mod makers here. A personal defs-only mod is save-safe to add, and mistakes are gentle: a broken entry logs a red config error at startup and is skipped, it will not break the game.

A Unique (Uniques & Mythics)

A UniqueDef is a fixed identity: a name, lore, a slot, and an authored affix loadout. Any gear of that slot - vanilla or modded - that rolls Relic tier can manifest as your unique.

Defs/MyUniques.xml
<IOUniquesMythics.UniqueDef>
  <defName>MyMod_U_WardensOath</defName>
  <uniqueName>The Warden's Oath</uniqueName>
  <flavor>Sworn on a wall that never fell.</flavor>
  <slot>TorsoShell</slot>
  <weight>1.0</weight>              <!-- optional: pick chance vs other uniques -->
  <indestructible>false</indestructible> <!-- optional -->
  <affixes>
    <li><kind>Stat</kind><stat>ArmorRating_Sharp</stat><value>0.25</value><greater>true</greater></li>
    <li><kind>Capacity</kind><capacity>Moving</capacity><value>0.08</value></li>
    <li><kind>Skill</kind><skill>Melee</skill><value>3</value></li>
    <li><kind>Aura</kind><aura>Defiance</aura><value>0.30</value><artifact>true</artifact></li>
  </affixes>
</IOUniquesMythics.UniqueDef>

Slots

Slot valueMatches
Headoverhead and eye cover
TorsoShellouter armor layer over the torso
TorsoSkinshirts, on-skin or middle torso wear
Legspants and leg wear
Utilitythe belt layer
MeleeWeapon / RangedWeaponweapons by class

Affix entries (the shared loadout format)

kindExtra fieldvalue means
Stat<stat> - any StatDef defName, modded includedflat offset (0.25 = +25% on percent stats)
Capacity<capacity> - a PawnCapacityDef (Moving, Sight, Manipulation…)capacity offset (0.08 = +8%)
Skill<skill> - a SkillDef+levels
AllSkillsnone+levels to every skill
Aura<aura> - Vigor, Might, Defiance, Prayer, Meditation, Fanaticism, Conviction, HolyFire, HolyFreeze, HolyShockaura magnitude (0.30 = a strong roll)

Add <greater>true</greater> or <artifact>true</artifact> to rank an entry (artifact implies greater). Relic and IsekaiStat kinds are not supported in fixed loadouts - the startup config check will tell you.

A Mythic (Uniques & Mythics)

A MythicDef adds a power on top of the unique format: a wearer hediff plus the name and description shown on the card. Point it at any HediffDef - one of the addon's existing powers, or your own.

Defs/MyMythics.xml
<IOUniquesMythics.MythicDef>
  <defName>MyMod_M_Dawnbreaker</defName>
  <mythicName>Dawnbreaker</mythicName>
  <flavor>The night lost, once. It remembers.</flavor>
  <slot>MeleeWeapon</slot>
  <power>IOUM_Power_Sanctified</power>          <!-- a HediffDef applied to the wielder -->
  <powerName>Sanctified</powerName>
  <powerDescription>Heals a permanent scar periodically; injuries heal much faster.</powerDescription>
  <affixes>
    <li><kind>Stat</kind><stat>MeleeDamageFactor</stat><value>0.55</value><artifact>true</artifact></li>
    <li><kind>Aura</kind><aura>Might</aura><value>0.50</value><artifact>true</artifact></li>
  </affixes>
</IOUniquesMythics.MythicDef>

Mythics default to <indestructible>true</indestructible>. An optional <artAuthor> field (uniques too) names the in-lore maker on the vanilla Art tab.

A Set (Item Sets)

A SetDef names the set, points at the hediff that carries the actual bonuses, and describes the capstone aura. The bonuses lines are display text; the mechanics live on your hediff's stages (one stage per worn-piece count - copy any DSLS_Set_* hediff from the addon's SetHediffs.xml as a template).

Defs/MySets.xml
<DiabloStyleLootSets.SetDef>
  <defName>MyMod_Set_Nightwatch</defName>
  <setName>Nightwatch Regalia</setName>
  <hediff>MyMod_SetHediff_Nightwatch</hediff>
  <totalPieces>4</totalPieces>
  <weight>1.0</weight>
  <color>(0.45, 0.65, 0.90)</color>
  <auraKind>Nova</auraKind>                <!-- Nova = damage pulse, Ward = healing field -->
  <auraName>Midnight Toll</auraName>
  <auraDamageDef>Stun</auraDamageDef>
  <auraDamage>6</auraDamage>
  <auraRadius>5.5</auraRadius>
  <bonuses>
    <li>(2 pieces) +10% move speed at night</li>
    <li>(4 pieces) the full watch: darkness is yours</li>
  </bonuses>
</DiabloStyleLootSets.SetDef>

A Rune or Gem (Runes & Socketables)

A SocketRuneDef defines the socketable's effects per host slot (weapon / helmet / body armor / other) and points at a companion ThingDef for the physical item (copy one of the addon's rune ThingDefs, they inherit IOR_RuneItemBase). ladderIndex places runes on the 30-step ascension ladder - three identical runes transmute into the next index at the Luminary Cube.

Defs/MyRunes.xml
<IORunes.SocketRuneDef>
  <defName>MyMod_SocketRune_Foo</defName>
  <label>Foo</label>
  <ladderIndex>31</ladderIndex>          <!-- gems use 200+, facets 100+, with isGem / isJewel -->
  <itemDefName>MyMod_Rune_Foo</itemDefName>
  <color>(0.8, 0.4, 0.9)</color>
  <dropWeight>0.5</dropWeight>
  <weapon><stat>MeleeDamageFactor</stat><value>0.06</value></weapon>
  <helmet><stat>GlobalLearningFactor</stat><value>0.06</value></helmet>
  <armor><stat>ArmorRating_Sharp</stat><value>0.04</value></armor>
  <other><stat>MoveSpeed</stat><value>0.03</value></other>
</IORunes.SocketRuneDef>

Slot effects may also be procs instead of stats: <weapon><kind>Burn</kind><value>0.12</value></weapon> (Ruby-style) or Stun. Facet jewels add <facetElement>Fire</facetElement>, <elementDamageBonus>, and <resistPierce> and ignore the per-slot blocks - a facet reads the same in every socket.

A Runeword (Runes & Socketables)

Defs/MyRunewords.xml
<IORunes.RuneWordDef>
  <defName>MyMod_RW_Nightfall</defName>
  <runewordName>Nightfall</runewordName>
  <slot>Weapon</slot>                    <!-- Weapon / BodyArmor / Helmet / Other -->
  <runeSequence><li>IOR_SocketRune_Myr</li><li>IOR_SocketRune_Onn</li></runeSequence>
  <description>Spoken only after dark.</description>
  <effects>
    <li><stat>MeleeDodgeChance</stat><value>0.15</value></li>
    <li><kind>Stun</kind><value>0.10</value></li>
  </effects>
</IORunes.RuneWordDef>

The word completes when the base item of the right slot holds exactly that rune sequence, in order, in exactly that many sockets. Runeword bonuses stack on top of each rune's own effect; set items refuse runewords.

Testing your content

  • Config errors first. Broken entries print red errors at startup naming your defName - fix those before anything else.
  • Dev mode. The Luminary Cube's dev tools include Make specific unique / mythic… and Add specific prefix / suffix… pickers - your XML content appears there immediately, no waiting for a lucky drop.
  • Balance guide. Compare against the built-in content: a unique's total loadout should feel like a great Relic roll, a mythic like a once-a-campaign event. When in doubt, copy an existing def and nudge.
Ship it. The same files work as a Workshop mod - your legends drop in anyone's game that runs the suite, and they inherit level requirements, loot beams, artwork, and cube handling for free.
