Sanctify & Desecrate

A Diablo 4 nod: gamble a piece of jewelry for a Divine affix - or watch it bind to its bearer as a curse.

The rite

Bring Legendary-or-better jewelry to the Luminary Cube, spend one Relic Dust, and ask the lattice to sing. It is a gamble:

OutcomeChanceResult
Success65%One affix is elevated to Divine ✦, a 35% chance the piece gains a third affix, and the piece becomes immutable.
Desecration35%The piece is DESECRATED. There is no undoing this.

A sanctified piece can never be rerolled, corrupted, cleansed, or sanctified again - the Divine affix is permanent, and so is the risk you took to get it.

Desecration - the curse

A desecrated piece binds to its bearer: it cannot be removed, cannot be cleansed, and will not come off until death. Its affixes flip to curse forms, and the rite always carves at least one fresh curse mark drawn from a pool inspired by Diablo 2's Necromancer curses and Elden Ring's rot, flame, and blood.

CurseEffect
Iron MaidenThe bearer takes a share of the melee damage they deal.
Mark of AmplificationAll damage taken is amplified.
DecrepitudeMove and work speed slowed.
Sundered WardAll armor ratings reduced.
Scarlet RotAn unhealing rot creeps through the bearer - lethal if it completes.
The Frenzied FlameChance to burst into flame when struck.

Curse UX

A blood-red curse mark floats above any pawn bearing desecrated jewelry - one mark per cursed item. Forcing a pawn to wear or pick one up opens a confirmation dialog first, and pawns will never put cursed gear on by their own choice (apparel score -1000).

Worked example. You sanctify a Legendary amulet with a strong Resonance affix. The roll fails (35%): it Desecrates, binding to the wearer with Scarlet Rot carved fresh. Now that colonist has an unhealing rot creeping in and a ring that will not come off - a real crisis born of a real gamble. Sanctify only when you can afford to lose the piece.

High stakes by design. Two-in-three odds of a permanent Divine upgrade, one-in-three of a permanent curse. Choose your pieces - and your bearers - carefully.
