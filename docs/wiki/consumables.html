Scrolls, Tomes & Potions

The consumables themselves - what each does, and the scroll-versus-tome economy.

Scrolls & tomes

Each function comes as a stackable single-use scroll and a 20-charge tome. Where both exist, an action spends a tome charge first, then falls back to a loose scroll.

ItemEffect
Scroll / Tome of IdentifyReveals an unidentified item via its Identify gizmo.
Scroll / Tome of Town PortalA colonist reads it; if not on a home map, the reader and adjacent colonists (with carried inventory) teleport to the nearest player home map's trade drop spot. Fizzles with a message if already home.
Town Portal range. Everyone within about two tiles of the reader is pulled home, carried goods included - a genuine emergency-extraction or loot-haul tool, especially paired with the Catacombs.

The four potions

Drinkable flasks, manual-use only (they never count as nutrition and pawns never auto-drink them).

PotionEffect
HealthHeals injuries (~50 total), bleeding wounds first.
Mana+0.5 psyfocus and clears entropy; no-ops with a message for non-psycasters.
Stamina~12h buff: +25% global work speed, +0.5 move speed.
Antidote~1 day: +30% immunity gain, +20% toxic resistance.

Crafting & trade

  • Potions - crafted at a Drug Lab after the IOSP_Alchemy research; trade under the Drugs tag.
  • Scrolls & tomes - crafted at a Drug Lab or the Luminary Cube after IOSP_Scrollcraft; trade as Exotic; tomes also appear in reward caches.
Worked example. Your delving party is deep in the catacombs, packs full of loot and one colonist bleeding out. They quaff a Health potion to stop the worst of it, then read a Scroll of Town Portal - the whole party and their haul snap back to base. A matching catacomb portal lets them return to that depth later.
