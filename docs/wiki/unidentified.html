Unidentified Items

Magic loot arrives a mystery. Its tier, affixes, and powers stay hidden - and its bonuses inert - until you identify it.

When an item starts unidentified

An item is unidentified when it is enchanted and Rare tier or above (or any Relic). White - now always plain - is never unidentified.

SourceIdentified?
Trader stock, quest caches, ground drops, catacomb chestsUnidentified
Colony-crafted gearAuto-identified
Anything a pawn is wearing / wielding (incl. raiders & recruits)Auto-identified on equip - so corpse loot is already identified
Why you never see an unidentified raider. Equipping forces identification, and pawn generation adds gear directly - so no pawn is ever seen in unidentified gear, and the loot you strip from a corpse is already revealed. Unidentified items come only from freestanding sources you pick up yourself.

While unidentified

A single choke hides everything at once - the item reports it has no affixes, so:

  • The affix card is hidden; auras are disabled; the item is excluded from the Luminary Cube.
  • Stat and skill bonuses contribute zero - the item is mechanically inert.
  • The info card shows a single "Unidentified" entry; the label reads "unidentified <stuff> <base>" with no stars or relic name leaking through.
  • The player cannot equip or wear it - the equip check blocks with "Unidentified (identify it first)."

Identifying

Select an unidentified item on the ground and press the Identify gizmo. It consumes the nearest non-forbidden Scroll of Identify on the map, or one charge of a Tome of Identify; it is disabled with a reason when you have neither. Once identified, the item reveals its full roll and becomes equippable.

Worked example. A quest reward cache drops three unidentified items. You keep a stack of Identify scrolls at base; reading them reveals a junk Rare you sell, a useful Legendary helmet, and - jackpot - a Relic with a faction-relic flag. You would never have known which to keep without identifying them, which is exactly the tension the system creates.
