Core Overview

How the engine turns every weapon and apparel into an ARPG loot drop - without a single per-item patch.

The premise

RimWorld gives each item a quality - awful through legendary - and that is the end of its story. Itemization Overhaul hides vanilla quality from every gear UI and rolls a parallel layer on top: a rarity tier, a hand of per-instance affixes, occasional auras, and rare twists. The roll happens once, when the item is spawned, and is stored on the item itself.

No patches required. A startup pass injects an affix component (CompAffixes) onto every ThingDef that is a weapon or apparel - including everything from other mods and DLCs. Materials (stuff, ingestibles, anything that destroys on drop) are excluded, so only equippable gear is affixed.

Reading an item

Select any affixed item and the inspect pane is replaced by a Diablo-style item card: the tier banner, the white base stats with their rolled values, the affixes with potency tags and - if you toggle the corner range icon - the range each one could have rolled in. The large info card (SpecialDisplayStats) reorganizes the same data into Status → Tier → Affixes → Unique effects → Special modifiers.

On the ground, the tier shows as a coloured label instead of the vanilla quality rating; Greater affixes append gold ★ stars to the name.

Where loot comes from

  • World spawns - trader stock, raider gear, quest rewards, ancient-danger caches: rolled when generated.
  • Crafting - the crafter's skill scales the tier; a master can rarely forge a Relic. See Crafting & Config.
  • The Luminary Cube - re-roll, upgrade, graft, corrupt, or recycle existing gear. See The Luminary Cube.

The endgame layers

Three deeper systems sit above the basic roll, each corruption-gated or relic-gated:

  • Corruption seals in one unique named effect that can never roll naturally - at the cost of locking the item forever.
  • Faction relics are the rarest Relic-tier items: worth a fortune and +50% to all stats, but their owners turn hostile.
  • The Cleansing reforges a corrupted item into a bonded relic of your own ideology, with one of twelve double-edged blessings.

Compatibility at a glance

  • Works automatically with modded weapons, apparel, and stats - the affix pool is drawn from everything loaded.
  • Vanilla persona / bladelink weapons spawn inert; the relic and cleansing systems own the bonding instead.
  • Optional addons read the same per-item roll and layer on sockets, sets, uniques, jewelry, requirements, consumables, and catacombs.
  • Character Editor round-trips the full affix state; combat and stat mods see the bonuses through the central stat pipeline.
