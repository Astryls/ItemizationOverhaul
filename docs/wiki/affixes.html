Affixes & Ranks

The per-instance bonuses that make two copies of the same item different - their kinds, their potency ranks, and the ranges they roll within.

Affix kinds

Every affix is one of a handful of kinds, drawn from a rarity-weighted pool. Stat affixes are the common fallback; skills, capacities, all-skills, and auras are progressively rarer.

Potency ranks

A numeric affix rolls at one of three potency ranks. The rank multiplies the rolled value and is shown as a tag (and gold ★ stars on the item name for Greater).

RankMultiplierWhere it appears
Normal×1.0any tier
Greater ★×1.2Legendary & Relic: 15% per affix, and a Legendary item is guaranteed at least one
Artifact ✦×1.5Relic-tier only: 30% per affix naturally, or via the cube

For integer affixes (skills and Isekai stats), Artifact does more than multiply - it pushes the value one step past the normal cap:

+Single skillArtifact reaches 6 (one over the normal ceiling)
+All-SkillsArtifact reaches 5
+Isekai statArtifact reaches 6

Roll ranges

Every numeric affix is rolled within a range derived from the receiving item and the global potency multiplier. The item card can show that range beside each affix - toggle the range icon in the card's top-right corner. Auras are the exception: their effect text already states their strength.

Content-aware drops

Rolls respect the item they land on, Diablo-style:

  • Weapons never roll armor bonuses, and ranged weapons never roll melee affixes (and vice versa). Neutral stats - labor, move speed, carrying - roll on both.
  • Auras only appear on weapons, chest armor, helmets, and shields; weapons only roll offensive auras.
  • Stackable gear never rolls ordinary affixes - it can roll the Replenishing package instead.

Tech-aware affixes

Affix flavor respects your playthrough's tech level, so a medieval colony never sees mechanoid modifiers on a longsword. The gate reads the higher of your colony's tech level and the item's own, live at every roll:

Gated contentNeeds
Mechanitor stats (bandwidth, mech repair, subcore work…)Industrial or later colony
Overclocked (work speed word)Industrial or later
Glittertech, Marine, of the Archotech (flavor words)Spacer or later
Item tech counts too. A genuine charge rifle that finds its way into a medieval game can still roll its native spacer flavor, because the item's own tech level raises the gate. Standard Crashlanded colonies (Industrial) see no change, and tech-advancement mods are picked up automatically. Toggle on the Affixes settings tab (on by default).

Prefixes, suffixes & the catalogue

Every stat affix belongs to a prefix or suffix slot and carries an authored Diablo-styled word ("Cruel", "of the Muffalo"). Words come from a curated catalogue of ~90 entries - browse the full Prefix & Suffix Table - and any eligible stat the catalogue doesn't cover, including modded stats, is auto-synthesised so it still rolls, just with a heuristic name. Each catalogue entry is gated to a tier band, so marquee stats escalate through named ranks as the tier rises (melee damage: Jagged → Cruel → Merciless).

Stats where lower is better - aiming time, weapon cooldowns, incoming damage, need fall-rates and the like - automatically roll as a negative offset, so an affix on one of them is always a benefit (e.g. aiming time -12%).

Rare-exclusive affixes

A dedicated pool of 12 affixes rolls only on Rare items - never Magic (too low) and never Legendary (capped out), in the spirit of Diablo 2's "Fool's" mod. They carry stronger value bands, so a lucky Rare can out-roll a Legendary on a specific stat.

Base-stat variance

Beyond affixes, every item rolls a ±15% variance on its own base stats - armor (sharp / blunt / heat) for apparel, and melee or ranged damage for weapons. Two otherwise-identical plasteel longswords will have slightly different damage. The variance is stored per item and is shown in the white-stat block of the card.

Worked example

A Legendary marine helmet. It rolls 5 affixes under a grand title. One is +Sharp Armor which lands Greater (×1.2); the card shows it with a range of 0.10 - 0.21, telling you this roll is near the top of its band. A suffix lands of the Surgeon, quietly granting the wearer +7% manipulation. The helmet's own base sharp armor also rolled +9% variance. This is a meaningfully better helmet than the next Legendary one you find.

You can disable any individual affix - stat, capacity, skill, aura, unique, or modifier - from the per-affix configuration window.
