Affixes & Ranks

The per-instance bonuses that make two copies of the same item different - their kinds, their potency ranks, and the ranges they roll within.

Affix kinds

Every affix is one of a handful of kinds, drawn from a rarity-weighted pool. Stat affixes are the common fallback; skills, all-skills, and auras are progressively rarer.

KindEffectNotes
StatA flat bonus to any stat loaded in your game (modded stats included).Higher tiers bias toward a curated "good" pool.
Skill+N to a single skill.Rare; each extra skill affix on the item makes the next far rarer.
All-Skills+N to every skill.Ultra-rare; carries a small cross-penalty so it is not strictly dominant.
AuraA radiating battlefield effect.At most one per item. See Auras.
Isekai stat+N to an Isekai RPG Leveling attribute.Only when Isekai Leveling is installed; favoured on the first roll.

Potency ranks

A numeric affix rolls at one of three potency ranks. The rank multiplies the rolled value and is shown as a tag (and gold ★ stars on the item name for Greater).

RankMultiplierWhere it appears
Normal×1.0any tier
Greater ★×1.2naturally on Ancestral & Relic
Artifact ✦×1.5Relic-tier only

For integer affixes (skills and Isekai stats), Artifact does more than multiply - it pushes the value one step past the normal cap:

+Single skillArtifact reaches 6 (one over the normal ceiling)
+All-SkillsArtifact reaches 5
+Isekai statArtifact reaches 6

Roll ranges

Every numeric affix is rolled within a range derived from the receiving item and the global potency multiplier. The item card can show that range beside each affix - toggle the range icon in the card's top-right corner. Auras are the exception: their effect text already states their strength, so a separate range would read as doubled information.

Stat affix value
value = baseRef · roll[StatRollFracMin .. StatRollFracMax] · potencyMultiplier · rankMult
baseRefa reference magnitude for that stat, so a +CarryingCapacity roll is sensible next to a +MoveSpeed roll
potencyMultiplierthe global "Affix potency multiplier" setting (0.25 - 3.0)
rankMult1.0 / 1.2 / 1.5 for Normal / Greater / Artifact

Base-stat variance

Beyond affixes, every item rolls a ±15% variance on its own base stats - armor (sharp / blunt / heat) for apparel, and melee or ranged damage for weapons. Two otherwise-identical plasteel longswords will have slightly different damage. The variance is stored per item and is shown in the white-stat block of the card.

Why it matters. Combined with affixes, base variance means you genuinely hunt for the best roll of an item, not just the highest tier - exactly like grinding a perfect ARPG drop.

Worked example

An Ancestral marine helmet. It rolls 4 affixes. One is +ArmorRating_Sharp which lands Greater (×1.2). The card shows it as +0.18 Sharp Armor ★ with a range of 0.10 - 0.21, telling you this roll is near the top of its band. The helmet's own base sharp armor also rolled +9% variance. Add a +3 Shooting skill affix and a stat affix, and this is a meaningfully better helmet than the next Ancestral one you find.

You can disable any individual affix - stat, skill, aura, unique, or modifier - from the per-affix configuration window.
